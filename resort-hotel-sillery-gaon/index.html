Best Resort in Sillery Gaon

Sillery Gaon is a picturesque village in Kalimpong which has recently become a very popular tourist destination due to its beautiful views of Kunchenjunga and Sikkim mountains.
Sillery Gaon is a cool mountain village nestled among the Himalayan hills, also known as New Darjeeling, at an altitude of 6,000 feet in Darjeeling district and Kalimpong sub-division is 26 km from Kalimpong and 99 km from New Jalpaiguri, amidst Damsang pine forests. . The name represents the plant so named that grows abundantly there. It is only a 4km trek from Pedong which is on the old Silk Road. The nearest stop is at Pedong which is a small hill town under Kalimpong division of Darjeeling district, Pedong is located at a distance of 21 km east of Kalimpong. With a total population of about 20 houses, mostly tribals, you will instantly fall in love with the place and its simple down-to-earth people.
A small village, but it offers nature like nothing else. One can wake up to the sounds of chirping birds and a beautiful golden sunrise. The view of Mount Kanchenjunga from here is simply breathtaking and can be seen very close compared to any place in the region. As the locals say, on a clear day leave Kanchendzong aside, you will get a view of Everest from this place.

How to Reach Sillery Gaon?

Sillery Gaon is around 100 kms from Bagdogra Airport and NJP Railway Station. You can book a private cab from these places to reach our homestay in Sillerygaon via Kalimpong. Also as an alternative option, you can take shared cars or buses from Siliguri to Kalimpong and catch shared taxis to Pedong. From Pedong you need to reserve a vehicle to reach our hometay in Sillery Gaon.

Sightseeing from Sillery Gaon

Ramitea Viewpoint:
The breathtaking longest view of River Teesta along with its 14 bends through the valleys is incomparable and amazing. Another view that you can enjoy from the Ramitey viewpoint is the confluence of the Teesta and Reshi rivers. If the sky is clear, you can also enjoy a stunning view of the entire region from Teesta Bazar to Majhitar in Sikkim. Ramitey View Point is just 3 km from Sillery Gaon.

Cross Hill:
Located on a lookout, it is also a sunset lookout. The view of Mount Kanchenjunga and other areas from this place is stunning. You can also have a clear view of the Tibet Autonomous Region from here. Cross Hill is highly revered as a place of pilgrimage for the local Christian community, as history says, in the 19th century, some evangelists began traveling to Tibet to spread Christianity. They never returned and the cross was erected in the hope that they would see the cross and know where to return.

Hanuman Top:
Once you reach Damsang Fort, you should not miss the small hike to Hanuman Top. Here you will find a Nice Statue of Lord Hanuman. The pristine beauty and serenity of this place is in itself unmatched. It also offers an excellent bird's eye view of Kalimpong town and the surrounding areas.

Silent Valley:
A vast wide green pasture, surrounded by tall pine trees and the only silence interrupted by the chirping of birds creates a place with a magical atmosphere. The downward trek through the misty forest and the sudden appearance of a green meadow will captivate you. The Silent Valley becomes a small body of water during the rainy season, once upon a time it was originally a lake.

Damsang Fort :
You can reach this almost ruined fortress after an hour's walk through the pine forest. It is about 4 km from Sillery Gaon. It is also considered an ideal place for bird watching. The road to this place through the lush green forest offers a wonderful view of the surrounding hills and valleys. This fort was built in 1690 by the Lepcha Kingdom, but very few ruins remain today.
