Best Resort in Lungthung

Located at an altitude of 11,500 feet above sea level, Lungthung is a rest stop with a fabulous view of Mount Kanchenjunga. On one side a panoramic view of the mountain and on the other side the winding road that leads to Zuluk. This place on the Silk Road is not to be missed for its defiant virginity. I was lucky enough to visit this place in the month of February when it snowed and every shade of white dominates this place. Lungthung, also known as Dhupidhara, is a peaceful village with a mesmerizing view of the eastern part of Sikkim. Perched on the lap of the Himalayan ridges at an altitude of 12,500 feet, this tranquil state is a must-see for the entire Old Silk Road region. The exotic view of Mount Kanchendzonga on one side and the dazzling sharp loops of Zuluk on the other surrounded Lungthung. The hilltop abode is such a fantastic place to spend time that provides an amazing view of the entire Eastern Himalayas. During this time, the village is all covered in snow; the only predominant color would possibly be white. From August to September, all kinds of flowers bloom here, resulting in a colorful landscape. During May to July, Lungthung is mostly rainy and misty and absolutely green.
How to reach Lungthung
New Jalpaiguri Railway Station is the nearest railway station while Bagdogra Airport is an option for flights. You can book a car to take you directly to Lungthung which is about 150 kilometers or hire a taxi from the station and the airport is also available. The shared taxi needs to change at Rongli to a local taxi that would go to Lungthung.
Best time to visit Lungthung
During this time, the village remains completely covered in snow and only the white color prevails, where from July to September, various flowers bloom, pouring in the picturesque picturesque landscape. During May and June and sometimes in July, Lungthung has to face rains and fog remains.
Viewpoints in and around Lungthung
Laxmi Chowk near Lungthung offers panoramic views of Kalimpong, Pedong, Lava and Algarah along with the sub-Himalayan ranges. Laxmi Chowk is like a garden full of rhododendrons along with mosses, lichens and ferns growing in abundance. The stunning Quekhola Falls is one of the most beautiful and visited places in the area. The sweet-tasting sight of water falling from the rough and cracked gigantic rocks from the green lush mountain above with the golden rays of the sun on the water is so poetic that tourists often stop and spend a moment passing by.

Sightseeing around Lungthung

Padamchen is another small village surrounded by dense forest of Himalayan range along with snow capped mountains. It is quite a popular tourist attraction due to its pleasant weather and spectacular views of Mount Kanchendzonga and other mountain ranges from Lungthung Lookout. Dzuluk or Zuluk is a famous tourist attraction near Lungthung. Viewpoints like Ganek and Thambi in Zuluk offer stunning views of the heavenly scenery of the great Himalayas and its magnificent snow-capped peaks that will instantly enthrall one.
A brilliant sight to see near this village is Kupup Lake. It is considered a holy lake by the locals. As the lake resembles an elephant, it is also known as the Elephant Lake. A fascinating part of Lake Kupup is the Yak Golf Course, which is the highest golf course in the world. Nathang Valley is a piece of paradise. An amazing valley with a breathtaking picturesque landscape is one of the main sightseeing spots near the village.
Luxmi Chowk: A visit to Lungthung is incomplete without visiting the famous Luxmi Chowk. You can enjoy a panoramic view of Kalimpong, Lava, Pedong and Algarah from here. In addition, the sub-Himalayan mountain range can also be observed from here. Luxmi Chowk is home to rhododendrons, mosses, lichens and ferns that grow here in abundance. You would be immersed in the beautiful chirping of birds. The road forks at Luxmi Chowk; one leads to Kupup and the other to the Gnathang Valley.
Padmachen: You can include this destination in your Lungthung itinerary. It is snow free but can be freezing cold during winter. However, Padmachen receives plenty of sunlight throughout the year. It is a great choice for bird watchers and butterfly watchers. Padmachen is also home to laughing thrushes, steppe eagles, buzzards, fulvets, wrens, buzzards and many other bird species. In addition, its dense forests are home to animals such as the Himalayan black bear, leopards and red pandas.when approaching because the rocks here are very slippery.
Original Silk Road: You can go through Padmachen Forest to the place where the fading trail of the original Silk Road is hidden. This is the very road that has seen a great increase in the horse trade for many years.
Heritage Forest Rest House: This motor-rest takes you back to the time when 'Sahibs' and forest officers swarmed the land. Observe the old wooden floors, fireplace and furniture here.
Rare Fauna: Lungthung is known for its abundance of rare wild animals. Here you can spot the red panda - the state animal of Sikkim. Besides, there are Himalayan Monals, Snow Pheasants, Blood Pheasants and Kalij Pheasants which dominate the fauna of the place.
